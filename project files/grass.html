Grass Pollen

Grass pollen is a common trigger for seasonal allergies, also known as hay fever or allergic rhinitis. It's a powdery substance released by grasses to fertilize other plants.

Symptoms of a grass pollen allergy can include: Runny nose Stuffy nose or nasal congestion Sneezing Itchy nose, eyes, ears, and mouth Red, watery eyes Swelling around the eyes Cough or sore throat Fatigue Headaches Hives or skin rash (contact dermatitis) Worsening asthma symptoms (allergic asthma) Grass pollen allergy season: Typically occurs in late spring and summer. Can vary depending on geographical location and climate. In some warmer climates, it can occur year-round. Common types of grasses that cause allergies: Bahia Bermuda Fescue Johnson Kentucky Blue Orchard Rye Sweet vernal Timothy Managing grass pollen allergies: Minimize exposure: Stay indoors when pollen counts are high, especially on windy days. Keep windows closed and use air conditioning with a HEPA filter. Shower and change clothes after spending time outdoors. Medications: Over-the-counter or prescription antihistamines, nasal sprays (corticosteroid or saline), and decongestants can help manage symptoms. Immunotherapy: Allergy shots (SCIT) or tablets/drops under the tongue (SLIT) can help build tolerance to the allergen. Consult a doctor: If symptoms are severe or persistent, or if you suspect an allergy, consult a doctor or allergist for proper diagnosis and treatment options. Important note: People with asthma should be aware that grass pollen can worsen their symptoms.
