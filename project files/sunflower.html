SUNFLOWER POLLEN

Several online resources provide images of sunflower pollen, particularly under high magnification such as a scanning electron microscope (SEM).

Characteristics seen in images:

Spiky outer wall (exine): The outer wall of a sunflower pollen grain is characterized by sharp, pointed projections or spikes. This feature aids in dispersal by helping the pollen grains stick to pollinating insects. Unique surface patterns: The specific ornamentation of pollen grains is used by botanists for plant recognition and classification. Male gamete: Each pollen grain contains the male gamete, a reproductive cell that, when fertilized, contributes to the formation of a new seed.
